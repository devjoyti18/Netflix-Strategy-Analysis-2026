This project analyzes over 15 years of Netflix library data to uncover shifting content strategies and global production trends. Using Python and Machine Learning, I developed a predictive model to forecast Netflix's content acquisition for the year 2026.

### The Series Pivot: 
Since 2018, the number of TV Shows added to the platform has grown 3x faster than Movies, signaling a shift toward long-term subscriber retention.
### Dominant Markets: 
The United States (Dramas) and India (International Movies) remain the largest content hubs, but South Korean content shows the highest upward momentum in recent years.
### Quality vs. Quantity: 
Analysis of genre ratings reveals that Documentaries are the most consistently high-rated category, suggesting a "safe bet" for quality-focused acquisitions.
